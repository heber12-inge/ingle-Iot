Welcome to the world of IoT

The Internet of Things (IoT) connects the physical and digital worlds, enabling countless innovations.

refers to all physical device systems that receive and transfer data over wireless networks with minimal human intervention.

What is IoT?

The Internet of Things is an ecosystem of interconnected devices capable of communicating and sharing data with each other.

Thanks to the integration of computing devices through the Internet.

For example, connected devices could change your entire morning routine. Pressing the snooze button would cause the alarm clock to automatically turn on the coffee maker and open the window blinds. The refrigerator would automatically detect groceries that are running low and order them for home delivery. The smart oven would tell you the menu of the day. You may even cook pre-prepared ingredients and make sure your lunch is ready. The smartwatch will schedule meetings while the connected car automatically adjusts the GPS to stop for refueling. The opportunities are endless in the world of IoT.

History of IoT

The term IoT was popularized in 1999 by Kevin Ashton, who sought to describe a world interconnected through RFID tags. Since then, IoT has evolved to include smart devices in almost every field.

It also arises from the technological evolution and convergence of two concepts: M2M (Machine to Machine), or in other words, communication between machines. SCADA (Supervisory Control And Data Acquisition). This is software typically used in industry.

Advantages and Disadvantages of IoT

Advantages

  • Greater efficiency in processes.
  • Automation of repetitive tasks.
  • Reduction of operating costs.
  • Better access to real-time data.
  • They allow you to connect various everyday devices, such as clothing, cars and household appliances, making things easier for people.

Disadvantages

  • Security and privacy risks.
  • High dependence on connectivity.
  • Initial implementation cost.
  • Lack of global standards.
